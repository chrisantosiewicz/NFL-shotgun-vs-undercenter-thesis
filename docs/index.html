Shotgun or Under Center: A Machine Learning Framework for Situational Formation Decision-Making in the NFL

Chris Antosiewicz | Syracuse University | 2026

1
Abstract — A concise summary of the research questions, methodology, key findings, and contributions of this thesis.
2
Introduction — Motivates the study of NFL formation usage, introduces the shotgun vs. under center decision, and outlines the analytical framework.
3
Trends in League Usage (2011–2025) — Tracks the league-wide shift toward shotgun formations, disaggregates pass and run usage, and identifies the EPA paradox surrounding the growth in shotgun usage.
4
3rd and Short Analysis — Examines the conversion rate gap between formations on 3rd and short, explores streak and repetition patterns, and quantifies the value of unpredictability.
5
Formation Switching — Analyzes how teams that switch formations across the course of a game differ in play type distributions and conversion rates from those that stay fixed.
6
Goal Line Efficiency — Compares touchdown rates by formation and play type in goal line situations, examines variance, and explains the shotgun run paradox near the end zone.
7
Modeling Formation Tendency — Builds logistic regression and decision tree models to predict formation choice, evaluates their results, and identifies why under center usage is harder to predict.
8
Predictive Modeling — Details the Random Forest architecture, QB quality score construction, the scramble contamination problem, the simulation framework, and the reliability lookup system.
9
Model Validation — Contextualizes R² values for EPA prediction, validates model performance across situational cells, analyzes systematic errors, and describes residual distributions.
10
Results & Key Findings — A summary of the most important numbers, tables, and conclusions from all sections of the thesis.
11
Limitations — A summary of the thesis' limitations.
12
Play Recommender — An embedded interactive Shiny application that takes game-situation inputs and returns ranked formation-play call recommendations with uncertainty estimates.
