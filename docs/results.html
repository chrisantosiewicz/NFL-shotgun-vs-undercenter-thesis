Results & Key Findings

A summary of the most important numbers, figures, and conclusions from across all sections of this thesis.

Important Insights from Trends and Descriptive Models

The fifteen-year trend analysis established that the NFL’s shift toward shotgun formations is real, sustained, and structurally significant — but its consequences are more paradoxical than the raw usage numbers suggest. Shotgun’s rise from 40.7% to 72.2% between 2011 and 2023 coincided with no corresponding improvement in league-wide EPA, and 2023 simultaneously represented peak shotgun usage and the worst average EPA year in the dataset, suggesting that widespread adoption has neutralized the formation’s advantages as defenses adapted. The streak and formation-switching analysis suggests that execution outweighs unpredictability — under center converted third and short at nearly identical rates on first and third consecutive usage, and the most telegraphed play in the dataset, switching from shotgun to under center and running, still converted at 69.4%, the highest rate of any formation-switching pattern. At the goal line, shotgun runs surprisingly led all combinations at 54.8% TD rate, likely inflated by quarterback runs and RPO concepts in a compressed field, but under center runs were the most consistent year over year with a standard deviation of just 2.4 percentage points. Shotgun pass was the clear worst combination at the goal line at 45.5%, yet coaches called it at nearly equal rates to shotgun runs despite the ~9% efficiency gap.

The descriptive models quantified what drives formation selection most. Third down makes a play nearly 15 times more likely to come from shotgun, and the two-minute warning cuts under center usage dramatically. The decision tree confirmed these intuitions structurally, identifying down as the single most important variable, followed by yards to go, seconds remaining, and yard line ranked in clear last. Under center usage was concentrated on first, third, and fourth down in short to medium yardage with a manageable score differential, and the near-even 50.1% to 49.9% shotgun split in standard one-possession game situations confirmed that genuine formation flexibility exists away from extreme game states. The tree’s ability to model threshold interactions gave it a 59.9% versus 40.1% advantage in third and short scenarios.

Formation-Play Performance Across Situations

The trained predictive models, validated against 2024-2025 test data, produced a systematic picture of formation-play type effectiveness across down and distance situations that aligned with football intuition in expected places while revealing non-obvious findings in others. On first and ten from midfield with an average quarterback, Shotgun Pass generated the highest predicted EPA at approximately +0.09, followed by Under Center Pass at +0.06, Shotgun Run at -0.02, and Under Center Run at -0.05. This ordering of both pass formations outperforming both run formations with shotgun variants outperforming under center variants within each play type, held consistently across most standard first and second down situations.

Short-yardage situations on third and fourth down represented the most consistent deviation from the pass-dominates pattern. On third and short, Under Center Run and Shotgun Run both showed positive conversion probabilities above 55 percent in the simulation, while the conversion probability advantage of passing formations narrowed substantially from its medium and long distance levels. The models’ reliability classifications correctly identified the Shotgun Run training sample on third and medium as insufficient for confident recommendations, preventing the system from generating the incorrect Shotgun Run recommendations that the pre-reliability-adjustment model had produced systematically.

The QB quality score added meaningful differentiation to formation recommendations. Testing the recommendation function with Elite QB quality score inputs produced materially different rankings than Replacement Level inputs in passing-heavy situations, with Shotgun Pass EPA predictions approximately 0.15 to 0.20 EPA units higher for Elite quarterbacks than Replacement Level counterparts in equivalent situations. This differentiation was particularly pronounced in third and medium situations where quarterback decision-making and accuracy under pressure most directly determine conversion outcomes.

Risk-Reward Profiles by Formation-Play Type

The 10,000-simulation EPA distributions produced by the stochastic framework revealed meaningful differences in risk-reward profiles across formation-play types that mean EPA comparisons alone would miss. Shotgun Pass distributions showed the widest variance, with 90 percent confidence intervals spanning approximately 3 to 4 EPA units in typical situations. Under Center Run distributions were narrowest, with 90 percent confidence intervals spanning 1.5 to 2 EPA units. This variance structure means that Shotgun Pass offers the highest ceiling and lowest floor, while Under Center Run offers the most consistent predictable outcomes.

The probability of a bust outcome, ranged from 8 to 16 percent for Shotgun Pass depending on the situation, compared to 4 to 6 percent for Under Center Run. The probability of a big play outcome above +1.5 EPA showed the reverse pattern, with Shotgun Pass generating big plays in 14 to 18 percent of simulations against 6 to 9 percent for Under Center Run. These distributional differences, surfaced explicitly in the recommendation output alongside mean EPA and conversion probability, allowed the system to communicate the full strategic picture of each formation choice rather than reducing it to a single ranking metric.
